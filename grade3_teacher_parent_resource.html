üß† Curriculum Structure & Design Philosophy

Welcome to the detailed curriculum guide! This resource provides a comprehensive overview of the Ontario Grade 3 Mathematics curriculum, focusing on the strands covered by the Brain Boost Arcade activities (currently B1, B2, and C1).

{/* UPDATED WORDING */} **Why the Interactive Learning Doesn't Follow Strict Order:** You might notice that the **interactive learning activities** sometimes mix expectations (e.g., placing B2.3 Estimation early) or group related skills together (e.g., B1.2 Comparison and B1.3 Rounding). This is intentional! The activities prioritize a **logical learning flow** for a Grade 3 student over strict numerical sequence. Foundational skills are often grouped, and related concepts are presented together to reinforce understanding and simulate real classroom assessment practices. This tree view below, however, presents the curriculum in its official, structured order for your reference.

B1: Number Sense

‚ñº
B1.1Read, represent, compose, and decompose whole numbers up to **1000** (e.g., standard, expanded form).Place Value, Standard/Expanded Form, Base Ten
B1.2Compare and order whole numbers up to **1000** using place value and symbols ($<, >, =$ ).Magnitude, Place Value Comparison, Ascending/Descending
B1.3Round whole numbers to the nearest **ten or hundred**.Estimation, Rounding Rules, Midpoint
B1.4Count to 1000, including skip counting by **50s, 100s, and 200s**.Skip Counting, Sequences, Patterns
B1.5Use place value when describing and representing multi-digit numbers.Place, Value, Digit, Times Ten Relationship
B1.6Solve fair-share problems resulting in whole numbers, **mixed numbers, or fractions**.Unit Fractions, Mixed Numbers, Equal Sharing
B1.7Solve fair-share problems focusing on **equivalent fractions** (halves/fourths/eighths; thirds/sixths; fifths/tenths).Equivalent Fractions, Numerator, Denominator, Ratio

B2: Operations

‚ñº
B2.1Use properties of operations (commutative) and inverse relationships (mult/div).Commutative Property, Inverse Relationship
B2.2Recall multiplication facts of **2, 5, and 10**, and related division facts.Basic Facts, Multiplication, Division
B2.3Use mental math strategies, including **estimation**, for +/- up to **1000**.Estimation, Mental Math, Benchmarks
B2.4/B2.5Demonstrate and apply algorithms for +/- whole numbers up to **1000**.Algorithms, Base Ten, Number Line
B2.6/B2.7Represent and solve multiplication (up to $10 \times 10$) and division (up to $100 \div 10$).Arrays, Repeated Addition/Subtraction, Grouping
B2.8Connect numerator to repeated addition of the unit fraction (e.g., $\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{4}$).Unit Fraction, Repeated Addition, Fractional Notation
B2.9Use ratios of **1:2, 1:5, and 1:10** to scale up numbers.Ratios, Scale, Proportional Reasoning
